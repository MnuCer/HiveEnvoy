Welcome to HiveEnvoy.

That missing communication tool to protect contracted pollinators. 


The adoption of best practices in crop management improves productivity in yields and quality.

Pest management and remediation is a very important aspect of crop management. Integrated Pest Management (IPM) practices may incorporate mechanical, biological, and chemical controls. Pesticides are routinely used to prevent crop losses and the specific chemical is specific to the crop and insect needing controlled. To reduce the risk of pest developing resistance over time, growers frequently alternate or switch chemical classes.

Another very important practice is managing the environment to support a healthy ecosystem for pollinators. While there is a wide diversity of native pollinators, much can be done to improve upon this environment. Increasing crop diversity and intercropping with pollen and nectar producing plants attract the pollinators needed for the production crop. Many production crops require the addition of managed pollinators such as honey bees.

It is a common practice for growers to turn over some aspects of crop management to other entities such as the tasks related to pollination, pest scouting, or pest mitigation. The grower may have contracts with three or more businesses to determine when and how some of these management practices are executed. 


HiveEnvoy aims to make that communication connection between the land owner, grower, pollinator, scout, and pest management. Not in the adversarial role as currently perceived but in a partnership to achieve better crop management. HiveEnvoy integrates many of those tools currently utilized into one platform and roles are selected and assigned based on function. All with an aim to protect those managed hive assets in the field.

Growers can digitally sign contracts with pollinator providers, scouts, and pest mitigation. Pollinator providers can mark the geographic location of deployed hives and have the common hive management tools in hand. Scouts mark and schedule fields for review and annotate needed remediation efforts. Pest management schedules remediation, documents chemical used, and navigates to the location. 

As the various tasks are performed, hive assets are mapped in the field and notification messages are sent between the parties. Giving the pollinator provider time to remove and return hives that best fulfils the need to pollenate yet protects the hive.
